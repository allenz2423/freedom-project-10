Solid State Batteries in the Automobile Industry

Why Solid State?

Solid state batteries are super stable and holds a lot more charge. This means that it would improve the mileage of a car by a lot.
They also could charge a lot faster than traditional batteries. It is estimated to be about 80% faster compared to LiPo batteries.
Cars require very reliable batteries to operate. A car crash could set a car ablaze and therefore having a high chance of killing the passenger.

Most if not all modern electric cars are using Lithium Polymer batteries. This poses many risks to the driver and the passenger. Lithium polymer batteries degrade very quickly and could be down to about 80% battery health in just under 2 year. This is a big issue because cars should be reliabled. When the battery dies, there is no way to recycle it efficiently. Here comes Solid state batteries. They have a way longer life span compared to lithium polymer batteries. They are also a lot safer than lithium polymer batteries. Upon being punctured, they will not explode. There are demonstrations that shows that you can actually cut a solid state battery.

Why should I care?

With more people adopting EVs, the more reports of lithium batteries failing in EVs will go up. This could easily be avoided. The automobile industry is going in the direction for exponential growth. You wouldn't want your car or someone else's car catching on fire mid drive right? Solid state batteries fixes this issue. With less battery pollution and less points of failure in a car, this gives the manufacturers more headroom to put more features instead of applying more failsafes.

How could software engineering be applied?

With a more stable battery, that hopefully means that there will be less failsafes required. This means that the programmers could remove or loosen the charging speed limit. Currently Tesla is working on a new LiPo battery that can charge 0-100 in 5 minutes, now if that were solid state, it could charge a lot faster than that, up to 80% faster. There would also be a lot less programming required for the voltage regulations and stuff like that because they can trust that the battery won't explode on them.
